Grade 3 also uses `gridSize: 5`.

### Lesson 7: Long-A Vowel Teams (Grade 2)
Patterns: `ai`, `ay`
- `ai`: rain, train, brain, chain, grain, main, pain, plain, sprain, trail, snail, tail, sail, mail, hail, fail, rail, bail
- `ay`: play, stay, day, pay, ray, say, way, bay, clay, gray, hay, jay, lay, may, pray, spray, sway, tray
Pattern hint: "When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking!"

### Lesson 8: Long-E Vowel Teams (Grade 2)
Patterns: `ee`, `ea`
- `ee`: feet, meet, see, tree, free, green, queen, sleep, sweep, cheese, creep, deed, feed, feel, heel, keen, need, peek, reef, seed, seek, seek, steel, teen, week, weed
- `ea`: read, beach, reach, teach, clean, dream, cream, lean, mean, please, real, seal, steal, treat, each, eat, heat, lead, leaf, meat, near, peas, speak, steam, team
Pattern hint: "EE and EA often make the long E sound!"

### Lesson 9: Long-O Vowel Teams (Grade 2)
Patterns: `oa`, `ow_long`
- `oa`: boat, coat, goat, load, road, soap, toad, coast, float, groan, loaf, moan, oat, roast, soak, throat, toast
- `ow_long`: snow, slow, blow, flow, glow, grow, show, know, own, low, bow, crow, flow, glow, mow, row, sow, tow

Pattern hint: "OA usually makes long O. OW can make long O too — especially at the end of words!"

### Lesson 10: Long-I and Long-U Teams (Grade 2)
Patterns: `igh`, `ie`, `ue`, `ui`
- `igh`: night, light, right, might, sight, tight, bright, fight, flight, fright, plight, slight, tonight

- `ue`: blue, clue, glue, true, due, sue, hue
- `ui`: fruit, juice, suit, bruise, cruise, recruit

Pattern hint: "Different spelling teams that all make the long vowel sound!"

### Lesson 11: R-Controlled — ar, or (Grade 2)

- `or`: corn, born, fork, horn, horse, more, north, porch, port, score, shore, short, sort, sport, store, storm, torn, torch, word, worn

- `oi`: coin, boil, coil, foil, join, moist, noise, oil, point, poison, soil, spoil, toil, void, choice, voice, joint, hoist
- `oy`: boy, joy, toy, coy, annoy, destroy, employ, enjoy, loyal, royal, ploy, soy, troy, convoy

- `ou`: cloud, loud, proud, found, round, sound, ground, bound, mound, wound, house, mouse, blouse, count, mount, shout, out, bout, gout, pout, snout, trout

### Lesson 15: Silent Letters — kn, wr, gn (Grade 3)

- `ought`: bought, thought, brought, fought, sought, ought, wrought

Patterns: `soft_c`, `soft_g`
- `soft_c`: city, cent, cereal, center, cell, circle, cycle, ceiling, cinema, pencil, dance, fence, juice, mice, nice, place, race, space, price, twice, voice, peace, grace, ice

### Lesson 18: Syllable Types — Closed and Open (Grade 3)
Patterns: `closed_syllable`, `open_syllable`
- `closed_syllable`: nap, bet, sit, hot, sun, bat, clip, frost, grab, jump, lost, shelf, test, track
- `open_syllable`: no, go, be, me, he, she, we, so, by, cry, fly, my, sky, try, why, hero, paper, label, tiger, open, robot

Pattern hint: "Closed syllables end in a consonant — short vowel! Open syllables end in a vowel — long vowel!"

### Lesson 19: VCE and Vowel Team Syllables (Grade 3)
Patterns: `vce_syllable`, `vowel_team_syllable`
- `vce_syllable`: pancake, sunshine, inside, outside, bedtime, birthday, everyone, somewhere, sometime, homepage, handmade, lifelike, moonshine, namespace
- `vowel_team_syllable`: rainbow, raindrop, footprint, classroom, afternoon, seashore, playground, oatmeal, sailboat, teapot, mailbox, daydream

Pattern hint: "Many words are made of two syllables — each one has its own vowel sound!"

### Lesson 20: Common Suffixes (Grade 3)

- `suffix_ing`: jumping, running, playing, reading, walking, talking, thinking, helping, cooking, eating, laughing, smiling, singing, dancing, writing
- `suffix_ed`: jumped, walked, talked, played, helped, cooked, laughed, smiled, needed, wanted, started, ended, melted, painted, rested
